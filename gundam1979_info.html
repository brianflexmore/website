Synopsis

ORIGINAL SERIES 1979

Set in Universal Century 0079, the series throws a teenage civilian, Amuro Ray, into the deep end when a surprise attack on his space colony destroys his home and forces him to pilot the Earth Federation's new prototype mecha, the RX-78-2 Gundam, just to survive. What follows is a gritty space-war drama aboard the warship White Base, populated by a rag-tag crew of civilians and soldiers fleeing Zeon forces, navigating political intrigue, shifting loyalties, and the brutal costs of war.

Primary Characters

EARTH FEDERATION

The Earth Federation is the governing body of Earth and its space colonies. In the One Year War, they represent the established authority fighting against Zeon's bid for independence. The Federation fields advanced warships, experimental mobile suits like the Gundam, and relies on the White Base and its young crew to carry critical hopes for survival.

Amuro Ray

Gundam Pilot

A teenage civilian caught in war, Amuro becomes the reluctant pilot of the Federation's prototype Gundam. Intelligent but introverted, he struggles with the trauma of combat while slowly growing into a capable soldier. His journey is central to the theme of ordinary people burdened with extraordinary responsibility.

Bright Noa

White Base Captain

A young officer suddenly placed in command after senior crew are killed. Bright is strict, serious, and often clashes with Amuro, but he matures into a reliable leader. Though not a mobile suit pilot, his leadership of White Base is pivotal in the Federation's survival and counterattacks.

Sayla Mass

G-Fighter Pilot

A medical student turned soldier, Sayla becomes an important White Base crew member and occasionally pilots the G-Fighter support craft alongside the Gundam. Secretly, she is Char Aznable's sister, adding emotional complexity and divided loyalties to her role.

Kai Shiden

Guncannon Pilot

Originally a cynical civilian with little interest in fighting, Kai reluctantly joins the battle by piloting the Guncannon mobile suit. Over time, he proves his bravery and develops from a sarcastic outsider into a dependable member of the White Base crew.

Hayato Kobayashi

Guntank Pilot

A childhood friend of Amuro, Hayato steps up to pilot the Guntank—a hybrid artillery mobile suit. Though quiet and often overshadowed by others, his determination and loyalty make him an essential part of White Base's defense team. His courage allows him to find purpose despite being thrust into battle.

Mirai Yashima

White Base Helmsman

Coming from a wealthy family, Mirai proves calm and dependable under pressure. She serves as White Base's pilot/helmsman and later becomes Bright Noa's trusted partner, offering stability and empathy amidst chaos.

Ryu Jose

White Base Pilot

A brave Federation officer, Ryu becomes a mentor figure for Amuro and the crew. He often pilots the Core Fighter and provides a steadying influence until his tragic sacrifice during the war.

Fraw Bow

White Base Civilian Caretaker

Amuro's childhood friend, Fraw Bow takes on the responsibility of caring for the war orphans aboard White Base. Though not a soldier, her compassion provides emotional balance for the crew during their struggles.

Primary Characters

PRINCIPALITY OF ZEON

The Principality of Zeon is a breakaway nation of space colonies that declared independence from the Earth Federation. Led by the ruling Zabi family, Zeon launched the One Year War seeking dominance over Earth and space. Known for their powerful mobile suits and charismatic leaders, Zeon characters often embody ambition, ideology, and the costs of militarism.

Char Aznable

MS-06S Pilot

Known as the "Red Comet," Char is a masked ace pilot of Zeon. Char is charismatic, cunning, and driven by personal vengeance against the Zabi family who rule Zeon. His rivalry with Amuro provides much of the dramatic tension in the story, symbolizing ideological conflict as well as personal vendetta.

Gihren Zabi

Zeon Supreme Commander

The eldest son of the Zabi family, Gihren is a ruthless leader whose authoritarian rule drives Zeon's war against the Federation. He is often compared to real-world dictators in his rhetoric and ambition.

Dozle Zabi

Zeon Military Commander

Known for his large stature and surprisingly compassionate side, Dozle commands Zeon's space fleets. Despite his loyalty to the Zabi family, he shows genuine care for his subordinates and family, making him a more complex figure.

Garma Zabi

Zeon Ground Forces Commander

The youngest of the Zabi siblings, Garma leads Zeon's Earth-based operations. Char Aznable manipulates him during his rise, leading to one of the early turning points in the series.

Degwin Sodo Zabi

Sovereign of Zeon

The patriarch of the Zabi family, Degwin initially sought independence for Zeon but gradually lost control as his son Gihren pushed the war to extreme measures. His conflict with his children underscores Zeon's internal fractures.

Kycilia Zabi

Zeon Intel & Tactical Commander

The only daughter of the Zabi family, Kycilia is sharp, calculating, and ambitious. She often schemes against her brothers while seeking to advance her own influence within Zeon.

Ramba Ral

Zeon Officer & Mobile Suit Commander

A veteran soldier who commands respect from both allies and enemies, Ramba Ral pilots the Gouf mobile suit. Unlike many Zeon officers, he values honor and fair combat, making him a sympathetic antagonist.

Crowley Hamon

Zeon Squad Lead

Zeon Squad Lead and the devoted companion of Ramba Ral. After Ral's death, she assumes command and leads a final, tragic assault. Her actions underscore the unwavering loyalty and personal sacrifices made by Zeon soldiers.

Mobile Suits

EARTH FEDERATION

Initially caught off-guard by Zeon's breakthrough, the Earth Federation countered with the prototype RX-78-2 Gundam. Highly adaptable and resilient, it demonstrated the Federation's ability to match and eventually surpass Zeon's innovations. Alongside the Gundam, support units like the Guncannon, Guntank, G-Fighter, and the Core Fighter gave White Base its unique edge. By war's end, the mass-produced GM provided the Federation with the numbers needed to reclaim balance and turn the tide.

RX-78-2 GUNDAM

The prototype mobile suit that changed the course of the war. Piloted by Amuro Ray, it combines cutting-edge firepower, armor, and adaptability, serving as the Federation’s answer to Zeon’s Zaku.

RX-77-2 GUNCANNON

A mid-range support suit, armed with shoulder-mounted cannons and rifles. Provides heavy fire support alongside the Gundam, forming part of White Base’s core trio.

RX-75-4 GUNTANK

A hybrid tank-mobile suit with dual cannons and caterpillar tracks. Though limited in mobility, it delivers devastating long-range firepower in early Federation battles.

G-FIGHTER

A versatile support craft that can combine with the Gundam to form multiple combat configurations. Used for transport, bombing runs, and tactical flexibility.

RGM-79 GM

The Earth Federation’s first mass-produced mobile suit, modeled after the RX-78 Gundam. Cheaper and less durable but highly adaptable, the GM served as the backbone of Federation forces in frontline combat.

FF-X7 CORE FIGHTER

A small, high-speed fighter that doubles as the cockpit module for the Federation’s prototype suits. Acts as both an escape system and a compact combat craft.

Mobile Suits

PRINCIPALITY OF ZEON

Zeon pioneered mobile suit warfare with the MS-06 Zaku II and its many variants. Mass-produced, versatile, and intimidating, the Zaku gave Zeon an overwhelming early advantage in the One Year War. As the conflict progressed, Zeon fielded increasingly advanced designs like the Gouf, Dom, Gelgoog, and the experimental Elmeth—each reflecting their push for military dominance and technological supremacy. These machines embodied Zeon's ambition and became enduring symbols of its power.

MS-06 ZAKU II

The workhorse of Zeon’s army. Cheap, reliable, and versatile, the Zaku became the symbol of the Principality’s military might and the face of mobile suit warfare.

MS-07B GOUF

Designed for ground combat, the Gouf trades ranged versatility for melee dominance. Piloted by Ramba Ral, it introduced the heat sword and whip into mobile suit combat.

MS-14 GELGOOG

Zeon’s late-war high-performance mobile suit. Comparable to the Gundam in ability, it came too late to shift the tide, though aces like Char piloted it with deadly skill.

MS-09 DOM

A heavy ground unit with high mobility thanks to hover jet engines. Famously used by the Black Tri-Stars, its teamwork and firepower posed a major threat to White Base.

MS-06S ZAKU II

A high-performance version of the Zaku II, customized for ace pilots like Char Aznable. Painted red for Char’s unit, it’s faster and deadlier than the standard type.

MAN-08 ELMETH

A mobile armor piloted by Lalah Sune, equipped with remote-controlled psycommu bits. It revolutionized long-range combat and showcased the fearsome power of Newtype warfare.
